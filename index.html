About the club

More than just speeches

Toastmasters International is a global nonprofit dedicated to helping people develop communication and leadership skills. We're one of hundreds of corporate clubs that meet inside a company — and one of the few that meets inside a government authority.

🗣️

Public speaking

Deliver prepared speeches in a low-stakes, encouraging environment. Get feedback from your peers and grow at your own pace through the Pathways learning program.

🧭

Leadership skills

Every meeting has a different structure and rotating roles — Timer, Grammarian, Table Topics Master, General Evaluator. Each one builds a different skill.

⚡

Table Topics

Think on your feet. Table Topics is an impromptu speaking segment where members answer surprise questions in 1–2 minutes. It's the fan favorite of every meeting.

🤝

Community

A small, tight-knit club means you get real face time and genuine feedback — not just polite applause. We're currently about 12 members and growing.

🎩

More than speaking

Every meeting needs a Toastmaster, Evaluator, Timer, Grammarian, Ah-Counter, and more. Each role builds a different leadership skill — planning, listening, facilitation, feedback — so there's a fit for every comfort level.

Members of Toastmasters clubs worldwide describe the program as transformative for their confidence and ability to find their voice — turning daunting communication challenges into a skill they can rely on in business and in life.

NJTA Reimbursement Program

NJTA reimburses 100% of your dues — approved by NJTA leadership effective 2024 and going forward. To qualify, within a 6-month eligibility period you must attend at least 3 meetings and complete at least 1 Pathways project. Periods run April 1 – September 30 and October 1 – March 31.

Submit the following to your supervisor as you would any reimbursement request:

  1. Cover letter (revise template as necessary)
  2. NJTA Employee Reimbursement Form
  3. NJTA Toastmasters Reimbursement Policy
  4. List of members eligible for reimbursement (shared by club officers after each 6-month period)

FAQ

Common questions

Do I need experience with public speaking?
Not at all. Toastmasters is designed for all levels, from people who are terrified of speaking in front of others to seasoned professionals who want to sharpen their edge. You set your own pace.
Do I have to speak at every meeting?
No. Members can attend and observe, serve in a meeting role, or deliver a speech — whatever they're comfortable with. Table Topics participation is sometimes open to guests and is always optional for new members.
What is Pathways?
Pathways is Toastmasters International's online learning program. You choose a "path" focused on a skill set — like Presentation Mastery, Leadership Development, or Persuasive Influence — and complete a series of speech projects to earn a credential. There are 11 paths available.
Can I attend if I'm not an NJTA employee?
This is a corporate club for NJTA employees and retirees only. Guests are welcome to attend a meeting to see what it's like, but active membership requires NJTA employment.
How do I sign up for a role or speaking slot?
Once you're a member, log into OnRamp (this site) with your email. Upcoming meetings will show all open roles and speaker slots — just click to claim one.
Is there a time commitment?
Meetings run about 60 minutes, twice a month. Beyond attendance, how active you are is entirely up to you. Signing up for roles and speeches is encouraged but never required.
Is dues cost reimbursable through NJTA?
Yes. NJTA reimburses your full membership dues if, within a 6-month eligibility period (Apr 1–Sep 30 or Oct 1–Mar 31), you attend at least 3 meetings and complete at least 1 Pathways project. Submit the reimbursement form to your supervisor like any other expense. See the Membership section above for details, or contact a club officer for the current form.
Do I have to be a confident speaker to participate in a meeting role?
No — most roles aren't about delivering a speech at all. The Timer tracks how long each speaker talks, the Wordmaster introduces a "word of the day" and tallies filler words. Each is a low-pressure way to get comfortable before tackling a prepared speech.
